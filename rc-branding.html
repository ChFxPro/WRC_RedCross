Red Cross Branding & Communications Guidelines

For all SparkPoint/Red Cross Project Promotions
Follow these simple, clear steps to make sure your materials look great and comply with Red Cross brand rules.

1. Always Use Official Logos

Classic Horizontal Logo (for general print and digital use)
Classic Horizontal Stacked Logo (alternate horizontal layout)
Classic Vertical Logo (for situations where a vertical logo fits better)
Classic Vertical Logo - Spanish Language (use only for Spanish or bilingual materials)
Tip: Never use homemade or modified logos. Only use these official versions. If you need another format or color, contact your grant manager.

2. Logo Placement & Clear Space

  • The logo must always be placed on a clean, empty background—no images or colors behind it.
  • Maintain clear space around the logo at least two times the width of one arm of the cross (built into most files).
  • Do not crowd with text or other graphics.

3. Logo Sizing & Proportion

  • You may reduce or enlarge the logo, but never stretch or squash it. Always keep the correct height-to-width ratio.
  • Use the right logo shape (horizontal or vertical) for the space available.

4. Color Standards

  • Preferred logo colors: Red Cross red PANTONE 485 (or 100% Magenta & 100% Yellow), gray wordmark PANTONE COOL GRAY 11 (or 70% black).
  • If using one color, all-red is preferable.
  • The black version is only for newspaper or all-black printing.

5. Required Credit Text

  • Every time you use the Red Cross logo, include this text near it:
“This [wildfire recovery] project was funded in part by a grant from the American Red Cross.”
(Adjust wording for your specific grant if needed.)

6. Accessibility Best Practices

  • Use high-contrast text and large, readable fonts in all materials.
  • Add alt text to all logo and graphic images for web or social media.

7. Required Approval Process

  • Send all new materials to your Red Cross grant manager for review and approval before publishing or printing.
  • Email drafts to cynthia.routh@redcross.org and joan.ward@redcross.org (allow 3–5 days for feedback).
  • For questions about logo use or special formats, contact BrandID@redcross.org

8. Sample Uses

  • Flyers/Brochures: Use the Classic Horizontal or Vertical logo at the top or bottom, with required text.
  • Web or Social Media: Use the logo in the header or footer. Add alt text and required credit text nearby.
  • Bilingual Materials: Use the Spanish-language logo for Spanish or bilingual audiences only.

9. Boilerplate for Press Releases

At the end of every press release, include:

The American Red Cross shelters, feeds and provides emotional support to victims of disasters; supplies about 40 percent of the nation's blood; teaches skills that save lives; provides international humanitarian aid; and supports military members and their families. The Red Cross is a not-for-profit organization that depends on volunteers and the generosity of the American public to perform its mission. For more information, please visit redcross.org or cruzrojaamericana.org, or visit us on social media.
